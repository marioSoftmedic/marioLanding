## The Chaos at the Front Desk

On Tuesday at 11:00 AM, a patient sent a WhatsApp message to the lab. The photo of the medical order was poorly lit, cropped, and the doctor's handwriting looked like ancient Sanskrit.

A human receptionist takes between 3 and 5 minutes to decipher that, cross-reference it with the pricing catalog, and reply with a quote. Multiply that by 200 daily messages. The front-desk team burns out.

## What Nobody Tells You About OCR

Everyone thinks the core problem in digital health is extracting text from an image (OCR). But reading "Cmplete blud count" (including the doctor's spelling mistakes) is useless on its own.

The real challenge isn't parsing text; it's parsing *intent*.

Is the patient just asking if the lab performs the test? Do they want a quote? Are they trying to schedule a home visit? If your system doesn't understand intent, you've merely moved the friction from paper to a chat screen.

## 60% Noise, 40% Booking

When we started processing this in Examya, we realized that 60% of incoming messages weren't direct booking requests; they were exploratory pricing inquiries.

If you treat every message as a booking request, you break the patient experience.

## Extracting Intent with ShuriStateMachine

We built `ShuriStateMachine`, our agent that takes that blurry photo, extracts the medical entities, and turns them into a structured object: 

```typescript
{ 
  intent: "budget", 
  patientType: "FONASA", 
  exams: ["0301045", "0302047"] 
}
```

## The Gotcha: Determinism Over Magic

Our first mistake was trusting the LLM (the AI) to map the exam and quote it directly. Bad idea. AI hallucinates non-existent exams or mixes up biochemical profiles.

The design decision that changed everything: we took away the AI's freedom to guess. The LLM extracts the raw text and vector-matches it against a closed FONASA catalog or the lab's LIS. If there's no exact match, it raises a flag for human oversight. Determinism over magic.

## From Call Center to Clinical API

A photo of an order via WhatsApp is the most universal (and chaotic) format in Latin Healthcare.

Converting that photo into structured intent is the key to real automation. Once you have a reliable JSON, you can query the LIS, apply business rules, and return a formal quote to the patient in seconds—without a human ever touching the keyboard.

The lab transforms from an overloaded call center into a clinical API.

## What's Next: Predictive Rules

We are currently refining the extraction of complementary diagnoses (ICD-10) from the same photo to enable predictive scheduling rules (e.g., automatically requesting fasting indications before the patient even asks).
