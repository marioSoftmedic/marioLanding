A clinic or laboratory's Data Protection Officer (DPO) has an impossible job: ensuring every piece of clinical data remains private, auditable, and traceable. The problem is that when it's time to audit a data access event, the IT team usually hands over a `.txt` file or a raw database dump full of incomprehensible logs.

With the arrival of Law 21.719 (Chile's new data privacy framework), this artisanal process is no longer just inefficient; it's a massive legal risk.

### The problem with raw auditing

Auditing who viewed which exam, when, and under what legal context (consent) is a nightmare if your infrastructure wasn't built for privacy by design.

If you rely on a developer manually searching server logs to piece together a traceability report, your RegTech shield is broken. It simply doesn't scale.

### Our decision at Fhirex

When we were designing the compliance layer at Fhirex, we made a strict architectural decision: take the human out of the raw auditing loop.

Instead of building complex dashboards on top of messy logs, we decided to orchestrate a **dedicated AI agent focused entirely on traceability**.

### The AuditEvent Agent

We built a workflow where the AI operates as a passive but intelligent observer.

Every time a sensitive transaction occurs in the API (e.g., a doctor downloads a PDF of results), the event is intercepted. The agent analyzes the payload, understands the context of the action, and automatically maps it to a native `AuditEvent` resource under the HL7 FHIR standard.

We don't save a text string saying "User X downloaded Exam Y". We save a structured, interoperable, and cryptographically signed object specifying the actor, the patient, the accessed resource, and the justification.

### The RegTech Shield

This approach changes the game. When the DPO needs technical evidence to respond to an ARCO request or audit a process, they don't have to ask IT for a favor. The evidence already exists, is normalized in FHIR, and is instantly available.

Law 21.719 is not an administrative checklist; it is an infrastructure challenge. And AI agents are the perfect tool to solve it without slowing down daily operations.

### What's next

Our next step in the Fhirex roadmap is connecting these `AuditEvent` resources to a preventive alert engine that notifies us of anomalies before they turn into security breaches.
