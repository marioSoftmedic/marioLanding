## The Illusion of Instant Quoting
It’s Saturday morning. You need to get a quote for some lab tests your doctor ordered. You take a quick photo of the prescription while walking, send it via WhatsApp, and... the bot tells you it can't read it. Frustrating, right?

## What Nobody Tells You About Medical OCR
Behind that instant response on WhatsApp is an artificial intelligence engine (an OCR) making a titanic effort. Its job is to separate a real medical exam from what looks like a doodle. And while the technology is incredible, the classic "doctor's handwriting" remains its greatest enemy.

## The Real Data in Examya's Logs
This week at Examya, we monitored why some automated quotes were failing. We saw cases where the engine returned a technical error (like a "401" token drop on retry, or Zod schema mismatches) simply because the prescription was cut off, there was a shadow over the text, or the focus was so blurry that the AI mistook "Glucose" for abstract art.

## What We Built: Accuracy Over Speed
To solve this, we designed Shuri (our WhatsApp agent) to be honest. If the photo is bad, instead of inventing a test you weren't ordered (and charging you wrong), Shuri stops the process and asks explicitly for a better photo. We maintain an architecture where clinical accuracy comes before blind speed.

## The Gotcha: The Camera Doesn't Matter, Contrast Does
The secret isn't having a thousand-megapixel camera, but contrast. The biggest gotcha we discovered is that a perfect photo taken from far away works worse than a cropped photo showing only the text. The AI needs the letters to stand out against the white paper, without the glare of the flash or your hand's shadow crossing the image.

## Zoom Out: Educating the Patient Beats Forcing the AI
Helping the AI read your prescription means that in 30 seconds you have the prices with your FONASA coverage codes directly on your phone, without having to go at 6 AM to pull a number at a lab just to ask how much it costs. It’s a team effort: you provide the good focus, the bot does the heavy lifting.

## What's Next
We continue tuning Shuri's guardrails. Every week it gets better at understanding strange formats, old clinic prescriptions, and blurred stamps. The goal is for the friction to disappear entirely for the patient.
