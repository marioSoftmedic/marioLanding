## The problem that unites two countries

In Puerto Natales, Chile, my team and I faced the same pain that now repeats in Quito: clinical laboratories with isolated systems, doctors losing time in bureaucracy, and patients waiting for results that should flow instantly.

The Ecuador difference? They have ConectaMed. A digital ecosystem that already moves data between labs, clinics, and insurance companies. The challenge isn't building from scratch, but integrating with what exists.

## The "don't copy, adapt" strategy

When we created Examya in Chile, we learned a harsh lesson: each healthcare system has its DNA. We can't replicate FONASA in Ecuador because their regulatory structure is different.

What we can export is our medical order processing engine. Shuri, our medical agent, already understands Chilean clinical language. Now we're teaching it to speak ConectaMed's language.

The secret lies in our FHIR adapter. That layer between Prisma + pgvector and external systems that allows us to "speak" with any platform without breaking our internal architecture.

## The expansion trio: Ecuador, ConectaMed, Examya

Imagine this flow:

1. A doctor in Quito orders tests through their EMR
2. ConectaMed receives the ServiceRequest and translates it to local format
3. Examya processes the order: we verify coverage, calculate pricing, assign laboratory
4. The patient receives everything in WhatsApp: from confirmation to results
5. The doctor gets the DiagnosticReport back in their original EMR

That's the dream. But building it requires something no one else has: our real-world experience with WhatsApp + FHIR + integrated payments.

## Numbers don't lie

In Chile, we process medical orders with 98.7% accuracy on first instance. Our average response time to patients is 4.2 minutes. And more importantly: we've reduced test abandonment by 63%.

## The challenges no one tells you about

International expansion isn't just technical. It's regulatory, cultural, and financial.

Regulatory: Each country has its own data protection laws. What works in Chile may clash with Ecuador's Organic Law on Data Protection.

Cultural: WhatsApp is omnipotent in Chile. In Ecuador, usage is different. We have to adapt our flow to their habits.

Financial: Health payment integrals aren't the same. Mercado Pago works differently in Ecuador. Stripe has other regulations.

## The advantage of arriving late

We don't copy. We adapt. Our FHIR adapter is the secret. We don't care about the external system: if it has an API, we can speak with it.
