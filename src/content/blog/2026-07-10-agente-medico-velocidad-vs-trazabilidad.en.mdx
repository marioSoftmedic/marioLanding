## The Illusion of Speed

The entire tech ecosystem is obsessed with tokens per second. If your LLM responds in 400 milliseconds, it's a win. But last Tuesday, while reviewing Examya's operations log, I realized something: a clinical auditor doesn't care about your speed.

## The Untold Truth of AI in Healthcare

If the agent processed a medical order for a "Lipid Profile" and scheduled a "Biochemical Profile", the problem isn't that it did it fast. The problem is that, in a black-box environment, you have no way to explain *why* it did it.

In healthcare, speed is a commodity; trust is the real bottleneck. Building in this space isn't about programming an OpenAI wrapper, it's about orchestrating trust (Trust Engineering).

## The Real Data from Examya

At Examya, we stopped optimizing solely for latency and started optimizing for explainability. Every time our Shuri agent reads a blurry medical order sent via WhatsApp, we don't just save the extracted text. We save the exact prompt used in that millisecond, the model's temperature, and the OCR bounding box over the original photo.

## What We Built: ShuriTraceLogger

We built a `ShuriTraceLogger` that injects every transition of the `ShuriStateMachine` directly into PostgreSQL. If a patient or a lab complains, we can reconstruct the agent's exact mental state at the moment of the decision.

```typescript
// The trace is more important than speed
await shuriTraceLogger.logTransition({
  orderId: order.id,
  fromState: 'AWAITING_OCR',
  toState: 'MATCHING_CATALOG',
  evidence: {
    rawOcrText: extractedText,
    confidenceScore: confidence,
    modelVersion: 'production-llm-v2',
    promptHash: hash(currentSystemPrompt)
  }
});
```

## The Gotcha: Data Explosion

The gotcha with this approach is storage. Saving all the forensic evidence for every inference blew up our database in less than a month. We had to implement aggressive TTL (Time-To-Live) policies for intermediate metadata and compress operational logs to cold storage after 7 days, keeping only the final structured JSON alive.

## Zoom Out: Evidence over Speed

Clinical AI isn't about acting fast, it's about acting with evidence. If your agent can't justify its reasoning step-by-step to a medical director, you don't have a B2B product, you have a weekend demo.

## What's Next

What's next on our roadmap is packaging this audit trace directly as a FHIR Provenance resource, so the lab receives the order and the "mathematical proof" that it was read correctly, all in the same payload.
