## 1. The Hook
When you build in healthtech, it is necessary to separate communication channels. Public comments on social media or messages to personal numbers are not the place to share photos of medical orders or data that identifies a prescription.

## 2. What nobody tells you
It is natural to look for the fastest way to say "I need this." However, social platforms and personal profiles are not designed to receive these types of documents. The separation of channels is not just technical, but an editorial measure to prevent medical documents from being exposed on public surfaces.

## 3. The real data
To centralize the start of contact, Examya’s only official channel is **+56 9 8328 6178**. We do not receive orders on social media, nor do we manage inquiries through direct messages to the founder’s personal account. It is not necessary to publicly announce that you have a medical prescription to begin the interaction; just using the official route is enough.

## 4. What you built
We set up a predefined message (*prefill*) for the official channel. By using the link, the conversation opens with this exact text:

```text
Hola Examya, quiero cotizar mis exámenes. Tengo una orden médica para enviar.
```

This is the starting point. Examya supports exam management, but this channel does not replace medical indication, and we do not provide diagnoses.

## 5. The gotcha
The design challenge is indicating where the initial communication should occur. My personal WhatsApp is not the platform. Keeping this strict separation between the founder’s channel and Examya’s official number organizes how we receive initial messages.

## 6. Zoom out
This is a pattern in B2C products: accessibility makes any chat look like the right one. Defining the use of an official channel simply establishes a clear boundary between social networks and the designated contact point, without promising response times or processing guarantees.
